# Contributing

Thanks for helping improve this list!

This is a **curated reading list**. Papers are gathered from arXiv and pre-scored on five dimensions
(novelty, applicability, rigor, reproducibility, insight); the published **S- and A-tier** selection is then
re-evaluated with a frontier model and **reviewed and approved by a human curator** at
[OHSE AI Lab](https://ohselab.com). The Markdown files are produced by that curation pipeline, so please send
feedback through issues/PRs rather than large hand edits — a maintainer folds accepted changes into the pipeline.

## What we welcome

- **Suggest a paper.** Open an issue with the arXiv link and a sentence on why it belongs (system trading,
  quantitative finance, or AI/ML-powered trading). High-quality, reproducible, insightful work is prioritized.
- **Report an error.** Wrong link, wrong title, mis-categorized domain, or a paper that should not be S/A —
  open an issue or a small PR pointing it out.
- **Improve descriptions.** If an entry's one-line description is inaccurate or unclear, suggest a better one.

## Guidelines

- One paper per suggestion, with the arXiv URL.
- Entries follow the format `- [Title](arxiv-url) - One-sentence objective description.`
- Keep descriptions objective (no marketing language).
- Be respectful and constructive.

## Scope

In scope: system/algorithmic trading, market microstructure, factor investing, statistical arbitrage,
portfolio optimization, financial econometrics, and AI/ML methods applied to trading (deep learning,
reinforcement learning, NLP/sentiment, LLM agents, generative models).

Out of scope: general ML papers with no genuine finance application, and non-research/marketing content.
